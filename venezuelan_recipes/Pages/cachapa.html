Simple Cachapa Recipe

Cachapa is a traditional Venezuelan delight made from sweet corn, cooked into a thick, golden pancake and filled with soft, melty cheese. With its sweet-and-savory flavor and comforting texture, it’s a dish that brings the warmth of Venezuelan home cooking straight to your table.

Preparation Time

  • Total: 30 minutes
  • Preparation: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups corn kernels (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon butter (plus more for cooking)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Queso de mano or mozzarella, for filling

Instructions

  1. Blend: In a blender, combine corn, sugar, flour, milk, butter, and salt until smooth but still slightly chunky.
  2. Cook: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and grease lightly with butter. Pour a ladle of batter and spread into a thick circle.
  3. Flip: Cook 3–4 minutes until golden, then flip and cook the other side.
  4. Fill: Add cheese on top, fold the cachapa in half, and let the cheese melt.
  5. Serve: Enjoy warm with extra butter if desired.
