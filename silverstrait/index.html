History Is Written By The Victors

Two hegemonic powers stand on opposite coasts of the Silver Strait, embroiled in a cold war. A high-stakes game of espionage unfolds within their arms race, both nations avoiding escalation due to one simple fact: neither the wizards and theologians of the Paragony nor the engineers and smiths of the Bronze Empire could conquer the other without suffering enough loses that a previously weaker third party could swoop in and conquer them in turn. Victory isn't enough; it must be decisive. So each side seeks to tip the balance far enough in their favor that they could break the deadlock and invade without making themselves vulnerable. The battle will be won before a single sword is crossed.

However, regardless of who wins, the historical record will reflect that the heroic saviors triumphed over the villainous dictatorship. There is no universal objective account of the past or present; every side has its own limited, subjective, and unreliable perspective.
