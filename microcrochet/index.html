Crochet is a method of making fabrics or items by using a crochet hook to lock loops of yarn or thread. Microcrochet is simply crochet, but smaller! Microcroceht can be utilized to even more detailed stuffies or art. The large amount of time and effort put into these tiny works of are are considerable: a single microcrochet animagurumi (handmade croceht stuffies) can sell for up to 30 to 40 dollars! This is more of a reason to learn microcrochet. The name "Crochet" is derived from the French term crochet, which essentially means 'hook'. The difference between crochet and knitting is that each stitch in crochet is finished before the next one is begun, meaning the stiches will stay, while knitting keeps many stitches open at a time, which can cause them to slip or become loose.

Microcrochet, a growing trend in the croceht community, is a branch of crochet that uses a set of techniques to weave very small fabrics. Microcrochet crafts can present very realistic effects and are known for their fine craftsmanship and quintessential pocket-sized artworks. Due to their tiny size, the styles presented are amazing, interesting, realistic, or super cute.
Microcrochet crafts are the most delicate and tiniest type of crochet, usually made using sewing thread and 0.7 to 0.3 mm crochet hooks. Microcrochet crafts are about the size of fingertips or palms.
