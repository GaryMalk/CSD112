Prologue

During WWII, tanks were essential to modern warfare in World War II, playing a central role in both offensive and defensive operations. Their mobility, firepower, and protection allowed armies to maneuver across a variety of terrains, from deserts to forests.

Yet, each nations that participated in World war II has developed their own unique style of design, as well as characteristics. I will be showing you some of the most iconic tanks from Germany, USSR, and Graint Britain. Those nations has played some of the most critical roles in the course of the whole war, and their designs in armored vehicles have definitely impacted the trajectory of it.

Germany

Panzer IV

With over 8,000 unit produced, Panzer IV is one of the most iconic tanks produced by Nazi-Germany during World war II. Panzer IV has many convenient features, such as:

  • inexpensive
  • easy to mass produce
  • versatile (the chasis has been used in many other models of tanks)

Panzer IVs, as a multi-purpoes platform, are used as antitank guns, assult vehicles, self propelled guns, but at times, can also take up the role of an armored personel carrier

Tiger 1

As fearful as the tiger may sound, the Tiger 1 tank produced by Nazi-Germany is definitely one of the most iconic tank from World War two, featuring impenetrable armor and pack-a-punch canon, which at the time, was a deadly enemy for the allies to face.

With more than 1000 build, those monsters roamed across the battlefield taking down enemies one after another. Thus, Tiger 1 has became one of the most iconic tanks from World war II

Jagdtiger

Not known to many people, the Jagdtiger is a Nazi-Germany castmate tank destoryer. Utilizing a slightly modified King Tiger (Tiger II) chasis, Jagdtiger is the heaviest armored fighting vehicle operated in World war II, across all nations. The gun mounted on the tank was able to out-range any ally tanks and penetrate through their armor like butter.

Due to its excessive weight and time to assemble, only a small number of Jagdtiger was actually used on the battlefield. Yet, it still encountered countless amount of mechanical error that made it almost inoperatable.

USSR

T-34

Another very famous tank from World War II but from the Soviet Union. The production of T-34 indicated the rise of Soviet Tanks, as it effectively repelled the advancement of German troops. The T-34s were simple, cost-effective, easy to use, making it very effectively especially when most of the Soviet Union's production power was lost due to German invasions.

Soviet T-34 tanks was the first design to utilize sloped armor in its design. The sloped armor enabled it to use less or same amount of material to get more armor.

KV-1

Before the mass production of T-34, KV-1 was the star of the show. With its thick armor and bigger turret for bigger gun, It served the Soviet Union quite well. But due to its expensive production cost, and T-34 performing better than KV-1 in almost every other way, it would soon be replaced.

KV-1 didn't leave the stage of the battlefield forever, since its chasis would later be used on another tank.

IS

The chasis of KV-1 was used to make the IS, which is classified as a heavy tank. The tank was needed in response of the Tiger 1 that was terrorizing the front line. A tank that can withstand a punch coming from the Tiger, and able to fight back.

There were many models of IS family that was produced, and they all really serve the same purpose, able to protect their homeland from the invasion of Germany, and battle result have approved of its capability.

Great Britain

Churchill

Churchill Heavy Tanks were used by the British as an infantry tank. It has many good features such as heavy armor, additional side spacing armor, ability to climb steeper slopes. Despite the fact that the Churchills are one of the heaviest tanks used by the allies, it went on to serve as a chassis for many specialist vehicles.

The design of the Churchills was deeply effected by its predecessors in World war I, it emphasised on its ability to cross difficult terrains, thus ending up on its longer shapes.

Click here to see more about Churchill series!

Sherman VC

Like many other ally countries that had to face the terror of the Tigers from Germany, the British also had to come up with something to counter the force of the Tiger, and that's when the design of Sherman VC was born, it was designated to hunt down tigers. and thus earning the name "Tiger Hunter"

The design of the Sherman VC was quite obvious, it used a similar chassis from the famous American M4 Sherman, but its mounted with a more powerful gun to be able to penetrate the armor of the Tiger.

Crusader

Crusader was the main source of power of the British Army during the early part of war. With over 5,000 unit assembled and used in many campaigns, it has made significant contribution to the victory of ally countries in the North African campaign.

Although Crusaders would be outcompeted by many other new modles of tanks, its chassis would go on and become a platform for many other supportive vehicle at the time, such as: AA-guns, recovery, communication, and etc.
