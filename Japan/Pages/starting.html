Starting in Your Application

Before you start


It is worth noting paths may be different depending on required tests and admission times, different universities and schools have different application periods. Also it is highly worth consdering applying to a language school first if you want to apply to a fully Japanese program, if you do this the pathway is still pretty much the same outside of a few requirments like certain tests not being necessary. Language schools while being expensive provide a good oppurtunity to adjust to the culture, and further explore oppurtunities, also greatly shorten the time it would take to learn Japanese if you are unfamiliar with languages like it.

Identify School

First you'll want to Identify where you want to attend and what are the requirments. it may be necessary to request additional information by contacting the school, it is recommended to do this step 12 months prior to when you are planning to start studying.

Take the Necessary Tests

Depending on what the required tests are IE JLPT, EJU, TOEFL, IELTS, SAT, IB, etc, regester for and take the tests.

Applying

Once you get your test results send the required documends and application to your chosen university or universities. This process can take 6 to 9 months so plan accordingly.

Mid-Examination

During this process it is likely you may be asked to complete a screening process, interview, or entrence exam. Sometimes this may require you to go to Japan, if this could be an issue check with the school before applying for any alternatives.

Acceptance

once you recieve your letter of admission, pay your tuition fee and plan your arrival in Japan. Planning should include housing arrangments, arrival on campus, and any further documents like insurence and payment plans.

Visa and Flight

By now you should have recieve a COE document, take it to a local embassy and apply for your visa, once you have your visa book your plane.
