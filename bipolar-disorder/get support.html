Resources for Bipolar Disorder Awareness

Explore these helpful resources to increase awareness, get support, and learn more about bipolar disorder.

1. National Organizations

These organizations provide educational resources, support, and advocacy for people affected by bipolar disorder:

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - Offers education, advocacy, and support for mental health conditions, including bipolar disorder.
  • Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance (DBSA) - Provides peer-led support groups and resources for individuals living with bipolar disorder.
  • BP Hope - A website offering articles, personal stories, and resources for managing bipolar disorder.
  • Mayo Clinic - Bipolar Disorder - Provides comprehensive information on bipolar disorder symptoms, treatment, and management.

2. Support Groups

Joining a support group can provide people with bipolar disorder and their families with comfort and understanding:

  • Find a Support Group - NAMI - Locate local support groups for people living with bipolar disorder and their families.
  • DBSA Support Groups - Offers peer-led support groups for individuals living with bipolar disorder or depression.
  • Meetup - Bipolar Support Groups - Search for bipolar disorder support groups and meetups in your area.

3. Educational Websites and Articles

Learn more about bipolar disorder through educational articles, personal stories, and expert resources:

  • Psychology Today - Bipolar Disorder - Provides an overview of bipolar disorder, including symptoms, causes, and treatment options.
  • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - Bipolar Disorder - Offers in-depth information on research, causes, and treatments for bipolar disorder.
  • MentalHealth.gov - Government site offering information on mental health disorders, including bipolar disorder.
  • BP Hope Magazine - A magazine dedicated to providing real-life stories and expert advice for living with bipolar disorder.

4. Books on Bipolar Disorder

Books can offer personal insights and professional guidance for understanding and managing bipolar disorder:

  • "An Unquiet Mind" by Kay Redfield Jamison - A memoir by a clinical psychologist who has lived with bipolar disorder.
  • "The Bipolar Disorder Survival Guide" by David J. Miklowitz - A comprehensive guide for managing bipolar disorder, written by a leading expert in the field.
  • "Madness: A Bipolar Life" by Marya Hornbacher - A memoir about the author’s experiences with bipolar disorder, exploring the highs and lows of the condition.
  • "Bipolar Disorder: A Guide for the Newly Diagnosed" by Deborah Serani - A helpful book for individuals recently diagnosed with bipolar disorder, offering advice and tips for living with the condition.

5. Social Media and Online Communities

Social media and online communities can be valuable spaces for connecting with others and raising awareness:

  • #BipolarDisorder on Twitter - Join the conversation and follow others who are raising awareness about bipolar disorder.
  • Bipolar Support Group on Facebook - A large online community where people with bipolar disorder can find support, share experiences, and learn from others.
  • Bipolar Disorder Subreddit - A forum on Reddit where people discuss their experiences with bipolar disorder, share advice, and support each other.

6. Awareness Campaigns

Participating in or supporting awareness campaigns can help spread knowledge and reduce stigma:

  • BP Hope Bipolar Awareness Campaign - An initiative to raise awareness of bipolar disorder and reduce stigma through education and outreach.
  • NAMI Advocacy and Awareness Campaigns - Join NAMI’s advocacy efforts to improve mental health services and raise awareness about mental health disorders, including bipolar disorder.
  • Time to Change Campaign - A mental health campaign aimed at reducing stigma and discrimination related to mental health conditions like bipolar disorder.
