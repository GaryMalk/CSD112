Supporting My Best Friend Through BPD: Learning What True Friendship Means

Author: Sarah Leslie | 32 | Washington | Published on: January 5, 2024

When I first met my best friend, I had no idea what lay ahead. We instantly clicked—sharing jokes, late-night talks, and countless adventures. Our friendship was filled with joy, but also intense moments I didn’t fully understand at the time. I’d often find myself on the receiving end of emotional outbursts or sudden shifts in her mood. There were days she would seem perfectly fine, and then, out of nowhere, a small comment or situation would send her spiraling. I didn’t know how to handle it, and I definitely didn’t understand what was happening.

As time went on, I began noticing patterns—sudden bursts of jealousy, emotional breakdowns over things that seemed trivial, or complete radio silence for days without explanation. These moments left me feeling hurt and confused, and I started to wonder if I could keep up with the friendship. I remember one time when she completely cut off contact after a minor disagreement, and I was left questioning everything. I didn’t know whether to reach out, give her space, or if our friendship was even still intact. It felt like walking on eggshells, and I had no idea what was really going on inside her mind.

Things changed after she received her diagnosis—Borderline Personality Disorder. At first, I didn’t fully understand what that meant or how it explained her behavior. But I realized that this wasn’t just her being “too sensitive” or “overreacting.” BPD was playing a major role in her emotional world, and I knew I had to learn more if I wanted to be there for her. I threw myself into research, reading articles, joining forums, and talking to others who had experience with BPD. Slowly but surely, I began to see things differently. I realized her reactions weren’t about me—they were part of a larger struggle she was dealing with every single day.

One of the hardest lessons I learned was how to communicate better. There were times I didn’t know what to say or how to respond when she was upset, which only made things worse. But I started learning how to validate her feelings, even when I didn’t fully understand them. I became more mindful of the way I expressed myself, choosing my words carefully to avoid triggering her anxieties. It wasn’t easy, and I made mistakes, but over time, we figured out a way to communicate that worked for both of us.

Boundaries also became essential. In the beginning, I thought being a good friend meant being available 24/7, always ready to drop everything whenever she needed me. But I quickly realized that this wasn’t sustainable for either of us. I learned how to set healthy boundaries—both for my own well-being and for the sake of our friendship. It took time, but we learned how to find a balance between me offering support and her understanding that I couldn’t always be there in the way she expected.

There were moments when I almost walked away. The intensity of her emotions sometimes felt overwhelming, and there were nights when I questioned if I could keep going. But then I remembered the friend I loved—the person who made me laugh until I cried, who stood by me through my own struggles, who had a heart bigger than anyone I knew. Walking away would’ve meant losing someone who meant the world to me, so I chose to stay. I chose to keep learning, to keep supporting, and to keep loving her through the ups and downs.

As we navigated the challenges of BPD together, our bond deepened. I started to see that our friendship wasn’t just about getting through the tough moments—it was about celebrating the good ones, too. The times we spent laughing, dreaming, and supporting each other through life’s highs and lows. BPD didn’t define her, and it didn’t define our friendship either. Instead, it became something we faced together, and it made our connection even stronger.

Being a true friend to someone with BPD isn’t always easy. It requires empathy, patience, and an understanding that their world is often a lot more complicated than it seems. But the lessons I’ve learned through this journey have been invaluable. I’ve learned the importance of unconditional support and the beauty of showing up for someone even when things get hard. And in return, I’ve gained a friendship that’s more resilient and meaningful than I ever could have imagined.
